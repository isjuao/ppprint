Secondary Structure and RePROF Predictor

A protein can fold into its native shape even when it is isolated from the cell, implying that the amino acid sequence alone determines the structure of a protein. On a lower complexity level than estimating the 3D shape of a protein, this discovery allows the prediction of secondary structure elements, using only the sequence. The structural elements as specified in the 8-state DSSP assignment can be simplified into three groups: helix (H), strand (E) and other states (O).
In ppprint, RePROF [1] is used to predict whether an amino acid residue belongs to a H, E or O part of the protein. As a locally forming structural element, helices require a minimum of four consecutive compatible residues to be formed. Thus, four amino acid residues serves as a minimum length for helical regions predicted by RePROF.
